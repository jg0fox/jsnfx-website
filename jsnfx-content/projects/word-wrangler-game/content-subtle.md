# Word Wrangler

A chaotic language game based on the Taskmaster game show.

I was asked to create a "fun" game for content designers to play during a recent team gathering at Atlassian. After talking with my amazing partner in language crime, Traci Wilbanks, we chose to create a task-based game inspired by the TV game show "Taskmaster."

I then asked Traci what kinds of materials I should print out so that everyone could play the game together. And she quickly scolded me and said, "We don't need to kill any trees for this game, Jason. We can do it with computers." And that's when my idea struck, and I chose to design a web app for a multiplayer content design challenge game.

## Game overview

Wordwrangler is a competitive writing game where players create creative responses to ridiculous prompts. A facilitator runs the game while players compete individually to write the funniest, most creative, and technically correct responses.

## Game flow

### 1 Setup lobby

Facilitator creates game, sets rounds and timer. Players join with game code.

### 2 Team assignment lobby

Facilitator assigns players to teams (optional). Creates teams if needed.

### 3 Round begins active

Task shows up on all screens. Timer starts. Players write responses on their devices.

### 4 Submissions

Players submit responses before timer runs out. Display shows who has submitted.

### 5 Judging

Facilitator reviews all submissions, picks winner, awards 1-5 points.

### 6 Leaderboard

Current standings shown. Facilitator moves to next round or ends game.

### 7 Reflection end

AI creates insights about team's responses in the style of the *Taskmaster* game show hosts.

## All 17 tasks

## Task 1: The Most Patronizing Error Message

**Category:** error_messages | **Time:** 3:00

Write an error message that is technically helpful but deeply condescending. The user has typed an invalid email format. Make them question their life choices while still telling them what went wrong.

**Judging Criteria:**

- Actually explains the error
- Level of condescension achieved
- Still technically usable
- Creativity of the insult

---

## Task 2: Forbidden Words Challenge

**Category:** product_copy | **Time:** 3:00

Write a 30-word product pitch for a password manager. You CANNOT use: secure, safe, password, protect, remember, hack, breach, encrypt, vault, or trust.

**Judging Criteria:**

- No forbidden words used (automatic 1 if violated)
- Still clearly about password management
- Compelling and clear
- Creative word replacements
