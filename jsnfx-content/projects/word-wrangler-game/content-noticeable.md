# Word Wrangler

A chaotic language-based competition inspired by the Taskmaster television series.

I was assigned the challenge of developing an entertaining activity for content designers during a recent team retreat at Atlassian. Following discussions with my exceptional collaborator in linguistic mischief, Traci Wilbanks, we settled on creating a task-oriented competition modeled after the television program "Taskmaster."

Initially, I inquired with Traci about what materials I should prepare for printing so participants could engage in the activity collectively. She promptly corrected me, stating, "There's no need to waste paper for this activity, Jason. We can accomplish this digitally." That moment sparked my creativity, leading me to develop a web application for a multiplayer content design competition.

## Game overview

Wordwrangler represents a competitive writing challenge where participants develop inventive responses to ridiculous prompts. A moderator oversees the proceedings while contestants compete individually to compose the most clever, imaginative, and technically sound answers.

## Game flow

### 1 Setup lobby

Moderator establishes the competition, configures rounds and countdown timer. Participants enter using access code.

### 2 Team assignment lobby

Moderator distributes players across teams (if desired). Establishes groups when necessary.

### 3 Round begins active

Challenge materializes on every screen. Countdown commences. Participants compose answers on their individual devices.

### 4 Submissions

Contestants submit answers before time expires. Interface reveals submission status for all players.

### 5 Judging

Moderator examines all entries, determines winner, distributes 1-5 points accordingly.

### 6 Leaderboard

Present rankings displayed. Moderator proceeds to subsequent round or concludes competition.

### 7 Reflection end

Artificial intelligence produces commentary regarding team performance mimicking the presentation style of *Taskmaster* program hosts.

## All 17 tasks

## Task 1: The Most Patronizing Error Message

**Category:** error_messages | **Time:** 3:00

Compose an error notification that provides technical assistance while being profoundly condescending. The individual has submitted an improperly formatted email address. Make them reconsider their capabilities while simultaneously explaining their mistake.

**Judging Criteria:**

- Successfully conveys the technical issue
- Degree of condescension accomplished
- Remains functionally practical
- Innovation in the critique

## Task 2: Forbidden Words Challenge

**Category:** product_copy | **Time:** 3:00

Compose a 30-word marketing description for a credential management tool. You are PROHIBITED from using: secure, safe, password, protect, remember, hack, breach, encrypt, vault, or trust.

**Judging Criteria:**

- Zero prohibited terms employed (automatic 1 if breached)
- Clearly concerns credential management
- Persuasive and comprehensible
- Innovative alternative terminology
