# Content design considerations for Netflix payments

Content design postulations, directives, and compositional elements for Netflix payments experimentation initiatives.

In this analytical examination, I synthesize insights derived from my concurrent functions as a content design strategist and UX research analyst to formulate a comprehensive assemblage of content design hypotheses, strategic guidelines, and compositional elements that could potentially serve as the foundational infrastructure for Netflix payments experimental protocols.

*This constitutes an externally-developed case study — I maintain no current employment affiliation with Netflix.*

[See Figma project files](https://www.figma.com/file/yMKK14OjzvgOVFRUVpkJ9p/Content-opportunities-for-Netflix-payments?type=design&node-id=1%3A615&mode=design&t=hyqGe3QYPkKXntHk-1)

At Baymard, we executed comprehensive usability investigations utilizing rigorous methodological frameworks. In collaboration with a multidisciplinary team, I authored an extensive analytical report synthesizing feedback from 185 participants employing the "Think Aloud" protocol methodology. From this exhaustive empirical investigation, I wish to emphasize six strategic recommendations that could potentially yield considerable value for Netflix's optimization initiatives.

All analytical insights presented herein were extrapolated from my professional engagement at The Baymard Institute and Chime.

## Six recommendations

1. **Elucidating Third-Party Payment Mechanisms:** Numerous participants demonstrated reluctance regarding third-party payment methodologies due to informational ambiguity—particularly concerning redirection to external domains. To amplify user confidence, consider providing preliminary explanations regarding how these payment integrations interface within your transactional workflow.

2. **Strategic Security Badge Positioning:** A more calculated positioning of the security badge, in closer proximity to the credit card input field, might substantially enhance user trust during the input of sensitive financial data.

3. **Visual Communication Enhancement:** Implement design signifiers such as border delineation or chromatic shading on credit card input fields. These nuanced visual indicators can frequently communicate security assurance more effectively than verbose textual explanations.

4. **Actionable Payment Option Articulation:** Rather than utilizing static labels such as "Credit Card", consider implementing actionable directives like "Pay with a Credit or Debit card". This approach transcends mere method identification and establishes the contextual framework for subsequent actions, thereby directing user behavioral patterns.

5. **Left-Aligned Textual Orientation:** Users demonstrated consistent preference for left-aligned content over centered alignment configurations. This represents an established usability paradigm that Netflix could potentially leverage, particularly within the payment transaction workflow.

6. **Tonal Considerations:** Particularly within payment scenarios, maintaining a neutral tonal approach proves crucial. Excessively playful or emotionally-charged tonal qualities can potentially undermine user trust. We must establish tonal equilibrium, especially when facilitating sensitive data transmission processes.

## Four hypotheses

I have developed a comprehensive set of four content hypotheses to complement my strategic content guidelines. I utilized 18F's hypothesis structural framework to clarify objectives and facilitate knowledge acquisition during the experimental implementation phase.

Here's an exemplification of the hypothesis content card configuration.

**We believe** that doing/building/creating **[this]** for **[this user]**

**will result** in **[this outcome]**.

**We'll know we're right** when we see **[this metric/signal]**.

[The 18F Design Hypothesis](https://methods.18f.gov/decide/design-hypothesis/)

**Hypothesis 1:** Explicating third-party payment alternatives we postulate that comprehensively elucidating third-party payment option functionality will resolve procedural ambiguity regarding subsequent workflow progression and can mitigate hesitation to initiate action for prospective Netflix subscribers. We'll validate our assumptions when we observe elevated clickthrough and completion rates on payment setup's tertiary step.

**Hypothesis 2:** Augmenting security perception we theorize that visually reinforcing text input fields during sensitive data entry moments and repositioning security badges will result in enhanced security perception for Netflix customers. We'll confirm our hypothesis through observing decreased abandonment rates or reduced temporal investment on payment detail entry interfaces.

**Hypothesis 3:** Conceptualizing payment options as behavioral directives we postulate that framing payment alternatives as actionable directives will more comprehensively prepare customers for upcoming task flow requirements. We'll validate our theory through increased clickthrough rates on payment selection interfaces and decreased bounce rates during payment data input phases.

**Hypothesis 4:** Implementing neutral vocal tonality we theorize that utilizing more neutral tonal characteristics within the payment experience will generate an experience more precisely aligned with customer expectations regarding appropriateness, thereby resulting in enhanced payment flow trust mechanisms.

## Figma content matrix

The matrix maintains organizational structure by content element typology and hypothesis categorization.

The Y axis identifies content element classifications, including heading, body text, button text, and additional elements. The X axis indicates hypothesis alignment, encompassing all four hypotheses plus a combinatorial option integrating all hypotheses.

You might find this component particularly advantageous because through a single component instance, you can generate all necessary textual layers required to populate a payment method selector interface.
